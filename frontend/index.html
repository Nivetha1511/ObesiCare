What is Obesity?

⚖️

Definition

Obesity is a chronic disease characterized by excess body fat that increases health risks. It's measured using Body Mass Index (BMI) and lifestyle factors.

📊

Statistics

Over 40% of adults worldwide are overweight or obese. This rise has serious implications for public health and quality of life.

🔬

Causes

Multiple factors contribute: genetics, diet, physical activity, sleep, stress, and medical conditions all play a role.

Adolescent Obesity

👨‍👩‍👧

Growing Problem

Teenage obesity is increasing globally. Early intervention is crucial to prevent lifelong health issues and mental health impacts.

🍔

Lifestyle Factors

Screen time, fast food consumption, sedentary behavior, and poor sleep habits significantly increase obesity risk in teenagers.

💪

Prevention

Regular physical activity, balanced nutrition, adequate sleep, and family support are key to maintaining healthy weight.

Health Risks of Obesity

🩺

Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes is strongly linked to obesity. Excess weight affects how your body processes insulin.

❤️

Heart Disease

Obesity increases risk of high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and cardiovascular disease.

🧠

Mental Health

Depression, anxiety, and low self-esteem are common. Social stigma can worsen psychological impacts.

😴

Sleep Disorders

Sleep apnea and other sleep issues are more common in people with obesity.

🦴

Joint Problems

Extra weight puts strain on joints, increasing risk of arthritis and mobility issues.

🚫

Other Conditions

Increased risk of certain cancers, liver disease, and other chronic conditions.
